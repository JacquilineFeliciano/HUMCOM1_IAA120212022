How to Cook Italian Pasta: A Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Step 1: Boil Water

    2. Start with a very large pot of water, about 6 quarts per pound of pasta.
    Bring it to a rapid boil.
    Tip: A pound of pasta feeds about 5 to 6 people.

  2. Step 2: Add Salt

    3. Put in a lot of salt, about 3 tablespoons.
    You taste the water, it should taste like the sea.

  3. Step 3: Add the Pasta

    4. Drop in the pasta while the water is at a boil and give it a gentle stir.

  4. Step 4: Stir

    5. Stir it occasionally while it cooks.
    Use the cooking time on the pasta package as a guide.

  5. Step 5: Taste the Pasta

    6. But watch out — it's hot! Sample the pasta at about 2 minutes shy of the indicated time.
    It should be al dente. You'll finish cooking it in the sauce.
    Tip: "Al dente" means "to the tooth," meaning the pasta should still have a little bite to it.

  6. Step 6: Drain

    7. Drain the pasta. Just be sure to save about 1 cup of the pasta water.
    If you have a pot with a colander insert, use it to drain the pasta.
    If not, drain the pasta in a colander set in the sink.

  7. Step 7: Removing Ravioli

    8. If you're cooking ravioli, use a slotted skimmer and gently lift the ravioli a few at a time.

  8. Step 8: Stir in the Sauce

    9. Add the pasta to your sauce and finish cooking it in the sauce. Give it a stir to coat.

  9. Step 9: Add Pasta Water

    10. Ladle a few tablespoons of the pasta water at a time until you have the right consistency to your sauce.
    Remember, the pasta will absorb the liquid. Tip: Pasta water helps to thicken the sauce.

  10. Step 10: Buon Appetito!

    11. Simmer over medium-low heat — it'll help the pasta soak up the sauce.
    Follow your recipe's instructions for finishing the dish. And that's all there is to it.
